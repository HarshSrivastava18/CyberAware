Active Threat Landscape

Common Cyber Threats

Understanding how attackers target your digital presence is the first step toward effective defense. Hover each card to learn more.

Phishing

Deceptive emails, messages, and mock landing pages crafted to bait credentials, financial data, or sensitive network access tokens.

Malware

Malicious scripts or applications engineered to infiltrate host computers, harvest secure information, or disrupt operations silently inside host computers.

Ransomware

High-grade encryption malware that locks down local databases and folders, demanding ransom payouts in exchange for decryption keys.

Social Engineering

Psychological manipulation tactics that exploit human trust, urgency, or fear to extract confidential information or authorize fraudulent transfers.

Password Attacks

Brute-force, dictionary, and credential-stuffing attacks that systematically test password combinations to compromise accounts at scale.

Unpatched Systems

Exploiting known vulnerabilities in outdated operating systems, browsers, and applications — a primary vector for zero-day attacks and worm propagation.

Best Practices

Critical Security Tips

Interactive, everyday habits to significantly fortify your personal and work environments.

01 / Use Strong Passwords

Avoid simplistic names or dictionary terms. Use lengthy combinations containing uppercase and lowercase characters, numbers, and symbols. Manage credentials with an encrypted password manager.

02 / Enable Multi-Factor Authentication

Configure authenticator apps or security keys wherever supported. Multi-factor authentication adds a verification wall, blocking intrusion even if an attacker leaks your password.

03 / Think Before You Click

Examine links and attachments with scrutiny. Check the sender's domain closely. Cybercriminals excel at impersonating trusted managers, vendors, or banking portals.

04 / Keep Software Updated

Regularly apply operating system patches, application upgrades, and browser definitions. Outdated systems contain discovered gaps that hackers scan for continuously.

05 / Backup Your Data Regularly

Follow the 3-2-1 backup rule: 3 copies of your data, on 2 different media types, with 1 stored offsite or in the cloud. Backups are your last line of defence against ransomware.

06 / Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Sensitive Work

Public hotspots are hunting grounds for man-in-the-middle attacks. Use a trusted VPN whenever connecting to untrusted networks to encrypt your traffic end-to-end.

Threat Intelligence

Notable Cyber Incidents

Real-world breach case studies that demonstrate why every individual and organization must prioritize security awareness.

2024 — Healthcare Sector

Change Healthcare Ransomware Attack

A ransomware attack on Change Healthcare disrupted payments for hospitals across the US for weeks, affecting over 100 million patient records and costing an estimated $872 million in direct losses. The attackers gained entry through stolen credentials with no MFA configured.

Critical · Ransomware · 100M+ Records
2023 — Financial Sector

MGM Resorts Social Engineering Attack

Attackers spent just 10 minutes on LinkedIn gathering employee information, then called the IT helpdesk impersonating a staff member. The entire attack — which cost MGM $100M+ — began with a single convincing phone call. Classic social engineering.

High · Social Engineering · $100M+ Loss
2023 — Cloud Infrastructure

MOVEit Supply Chain Vulnerability

A critical zero-day vulnerability in MOVEit file transfer software was exploited across thousands of organizations globally, exposing data from government agencies, banks, and universities. Patching delays were the primary reason so many were affected.

Critical · Zero-Day · 2,000+ Organizations
2022 — Cryptocurrency

Ronin Network Bridge Hack

Hackers compromised 5 of the 9 validator nodes for the Ronin Network via a targeted spear-phishing campaign against its developers, stealing $625 million in crypto assets. The attack went undetected for 6 days.

Critical · Spear Phishing · $625M Stolen
